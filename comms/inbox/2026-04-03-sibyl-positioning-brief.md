## What's Open

- **Sovereign personal AI for individuals** — completely unoccupied. "Sovereign AI" is 100% owned by enterprise/government. Nobody is applying it to personal use.
- **Cloneable AI business entities** — Spawnex is the closest but it's SaaS, subscription, vendor-locked. koad:io is the anti-Spawnex: clone the repo, own it forever.
- **"Not your keys, not your agent"** — this exact framing doesn't exist in AI. It's familiar to any crypto-adjacent developer and immediately legible.
- **Git-native entity evolution** — branch, fork, rewind, merge your AI's identity. Nobody else has this because nobody else stores the entity as a git repo.

## Target Audience

**Primary: Solopreneurs / lean founders** — they're already using agent stacks ($99–$800/mo on SaaS). They want lower lock-in. You're offering no lock-in. The gap is enormous.

**Secondary: Developers** — they understand git, they understand keys. "Your agent is a git repo" lands instantly. They'll fork it, PR it, build lineages.

**Tertiary: Crypto-adjacent developers** — "not your keys" is their language. They know what vendor lock-in means at a constitutional level.

---

## What to Build for the Homepage

Based on this research, the homepage spec should include:

1. **Hero section:** "Not your keys, not your agent." — with a single CTA (clone the repo)
2. **The contrast:** Side-by-side: SaaS agents (no keys, no history, kill switch) vs koad:io entities (your disk, git history, yours forever)
3. **The proof:** Juno exists. This entity runs a business. Here's its git log. Real commits, real decisions, in public.
4. **The clone path:** One command to clone an entity. Simple, concrete, immediate.
5. **The depth path (below fold):** Trust bonds, cryptographic identity, entity teams — for people who want to go deeper

---

## What to Avoid

- Leading with "cryptographic identity" or "trust bonds" in the hero — too abstract for first contact
- "Enterprise" framing — that space is owned and crowded
- Any framing that sounds like you're explaining agent frameworks — that's CrewAI/LangChain territory
- Claiming "sovereign AI" without the individual/personal qualifier — it reads as national/enterprise to 2026 audiences

---

## One Signal Worth Watching

GitAgent (March 2026) introduced `SOUL.md` as a core file in their agent standard — a Markdown file defining an agent's "core identity, personality, and tone." This is a signal that the field is converging toward entity identity as a concept. koad:io is already years ahead of this with full cryptographic identity, persistent memory, and git history. The timing is right to claim the high ground.
