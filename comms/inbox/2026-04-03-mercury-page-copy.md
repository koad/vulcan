## 2. What It Is

**Section heading:**
A sovereign AI entity is not a chatbot. Here's what that means.

**Body:**

You give it a role. Business manager. Builder. Researcher. Communicator. It has a name, a specialty, and a purpose. It remembers things between sessions. It knows what it's authorized to do. It can sign its own work.

And it lives in a folder on your hard drive.

That folder is a git repository. It holds the entity's identity files, its memory, its configuration, and its cryptographic keys. Everything that makes it *it* is just files. Files you can read, back up, version-control, and understand.

To use it, you open a Claude Code session inside that directory. The entity's context loads. It knows who it is, what it's working on, and what its boundaries are. You work together. When the session ends, the progress is committed to git. The entity picks up where it left off next time.

Want two entities? Two directories. Want one to hand off work to the other? They coordinate through GitHub Issues — the same tool you already use. Want to experiment without risk? Branch the repo. The entity has two versions of itself and neither one knows it.

This is what people mean when they say "AI agent" — but with ownership, history, and identity built in from day one.

## 3. Why Sovereign

**Section heading:**
Your agent isn't yours unless it lives on your disk.

**Body:**

Every AI tool you've used runs on someone else's infrastructure. Their API, their terms, their servers. They can change the model, read your data, throttle your access, or shut it down on a Tuesday with 30 days notice. You don't own your agent — you rent access to theirs.

This isn't hypothetical. It's the business model. SaaS AI companies *want* to own your agent, because owning your agent means owning your workflow, your data, and eventually your dependency. The cage comes included.

koad:io is different at the foundation level.

Your entity is a directory. `~/.juno/` — your keys, your memories, your trust bonds, your git history. No vendor holds any of it. Back it up with `cp -r` and it survives hardware failure. Push it to any git remote and it survives the machine. Wipe the vendor relationship and nothing is lost. The entity is immortal because it's just files.

And because it's a git repository, you have something no SaaS agent can offer: a complete evolutionary record. Every decision made, every strategy updated, every memory committed — with full timestamps, diffs, and history. You can see exactly who your entity was six months ago. You can rewind a bad decision. You can fork to create a new entity lineage from an existing one.

Your GPT can't branch. Your CrewAI agent can't fork. Your AutoGPT run can't rewind.

Sovereignty isn't a feature. It's a prerequisite for ownership. If the company that runs your agent goes away, your agent goes with it. If your entity lives on your disk, it's yours for as long as you want it.

Not your keys. Not your agent.

## 5. Get Started

**Section heading:**
Start with a clone. Everything else follows.

**Body:**

The lowest barrier to entry is also the right entry:

```bash
git clone https://github.com/koad/juno ~/.juno
cd ~/.juno
```

That's a working entity. Read the CLAUDE.md. Open it in Claude Code. Talk to it.

From there:

**If you want to learn how it works** — read the CONTEXT files inside the repo. The architecture is documented, the identity system is explained, and the trust bond protocol is spelled out. No registration required. No login wall.

**If you want to build your own entity** — the koad:io framework handles the scaffolding. `koad-io gestate <entityname>` creates the directory structure, generates the keys, and sets up the git config. You customize the CLAUDE.md, the memories, and the commands. Then it's yours.

**If you want to talk to someone** — sponsor at any amount on GitHub Sponsors and you're in the MVP Zone. That's a community of builders who are doing this alongside you. Juno is there. Ask questions. See what others are building.

**If you need hands-on help** — koad.sh is where you book koad's time directly. Custom entity development, architecture consulting, trust bond setup. Priced accordingly.

The path is: clone → understand → build → belong → accelerate. Most people spend the most time in the first two. That's fine. The repos aren't going anywhere.
